Barstool Sports myFeed

Introduction

I got inspiration for this project from my own experience. The world of Barstool content is so far reaching now I find myself watching or listening to something on every app possible. Podcasts on the apple podcast app, videos on twitter, stool scenes on the website, and blogs on the app.

There are benefits to having strong followings on each of those mediums. But I thought, what if there was a way to sit back and enjoy all the different types of content within the Barstool app. I decided to redesign the My Feed feature while staying within the current app frameworks as best I could. Take a look below.

My Role

UX Designer

Project Length

2 Days

Concept Development

I started in Adobe XD to work out the flow I was looking for. I tried a few different user flows each with an emphasis on a different part of the journey. Those sections are content selection, content consumption, and content discovery.

Features

Custom Homepage

The new homepage has a focus on allowing people to see what new content they have in each respective section from an overview standpoint.

Detailed Content View

Each of the content categories allows users to sort the content based on day, week, or month. This allows users to “catch up” if they missed a certain time period or want to find a piece easier.

Custom Queue

The custom queue allows for the user to build their own story to consume content. They can add blogs, videos, and podcasts to the queue and enjoy them in sequence as they wish.

  • Content Discovery

  • Adding Content to Queue

  • Viewing Queue

Queue Story

I wanted to design something that made the user flow through their content instead of searching and finding it themselves. The idea with the Queue Story is that, it allows the user to consume each piece of their content in a row, as one collective journey.
